flooding and drainage issue
  • Heavy Rainfall: Intense rainfall, particularly during the northeast monsoon, overwhelms the city's drainage capacity.
  • Rapid Urbanization & Concretization: The construction of concrete structures and buildings on vast tracts of land reduces the natural absorption of rainwater, increasing surface runoff
  • Poor Drainage Infrastructure: Inadequate design, construction, and maintenance of drainage channels and systems lead to blockages by debris and a reduced capacity to handle heavy Rainfall
  • South Chennai: Areas like Velachery, Adyar, Perumbakkam, and Pallikaranai are consistently vulnerable to flooding.
  • Loss of Life and Economic Damage: Floods have caused significant loss of life and substantial economic losses, as seen in the 2015 floods
  • Environmental Impact: Damage to crops, loss of topsoil, and harm to livestock are potential consequences for the surrounding agricultural regions
